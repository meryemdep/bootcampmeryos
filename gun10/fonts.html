Whales are large marine mammals belonging to the cetacean family, which includes dolphins and porpoises. They are divided into two main types: baleen whales and toothed whales. Baleen whales, like the blue whale, use baleen plates to filter small organisms like krill from seawater. Toothed whales, such as sperm whales and orcas, hunt fish and squid. Whales are known for their intelligence, complex social structures, and communication through songs and echolocation. They play a vital role in maintaining marine ecosystems, contributing to nutrient cycling and carbon storage, and many species are protected due to their endangered status.
