They Have No Brain, Heart, or Bones.Jellyfish are incredibly simple creatures. They have no brain to control their movements, no heart to pump blood, and no bones. Instead, they rely on a network of nerves called a "nerve net" to detect changes in their environment.

Jellyfish are among the oldest living creatures on Earth, existing for over 500 million years, predating even dinosaurs! Some species, like the Turritopsis dohrnii, are even referred to as "immortal jellyfish" because they have the ability to reverse their life cycle. When injured or stressed, they can revert their cells to an earlier stage, essentially allowing them to start their life over again! This makes them one of the few creatures capable of theoretically living forever.

What Are They?

A jellyfish is a soft-bodied, transparent sea creature with an umbrella-shaped body and long, trailing tentacles. It is made mostly of water and uses its tentacles to sting and capture prey.
